Presso — Logo Concepts

5 creative directions · Green-White palette · Minimal & Clean

1

The Droplet Press

A water droplet being pressed flat — clean clothes, freshness, pressure

App icon
Why this works: "Presso" literally means press — this icon shows a droplet being pressed, capturing both the cleaning (water) and the pressing (flat iron) aspects. The sparkle dots add freshness. Extremely minimal, reads well at small sizes.
2

The Folded P

Letter P shaped like a neatly folded garment — precision, care, craft

App icon
Why this works: The "P" doubles as a folded shirt seen from above — the horizontal lines are fold creases. The leaf accent nods to eco-friendliness. This is the strongest "monogram" option — works standalone on app icons without any wordmark.
3

The Spin Cycle

Abstract spinning motion — speed, efficiency, modern tech

App icon
Why this works: Signals "tech-enabled laundry" rather than "traditional dhobi". The spin arcs feel dynamic, the droplet centers it on cleaning. Most abstract of the set — better for a brand that wants to grow beyond laundry (cleaning, shoe care, etc). Risk: might feel generic without the wordmark.
4

Shirt Origami

A shirt folded into a geometric diamond — precision, premium, Japanese craft

App icon
Why this works: The diamond shape says "premium" — like a jeweler's cut. But on second look you see a shirt with collar, buttons, and fold line. Dual reading = memorable. This is the one I'd pick if Presso wants to position as the premium option vs cheap competitors.
5

The Green Hanger

Hanger merged with a leaf — eco-conscious, fresh, natural care

App icon
Why this works: If Presso ever markets eco-friendly detergents, water-recycling, or green packaging, this mark earns you instant credibility without saying a word. The leaf veins double as the hanger's symmetry lines — one symbol, two readings. Best fit if sustainability is part of the pitch.
